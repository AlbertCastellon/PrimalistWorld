# Classes and Subclasses

## Classes
Classes are a defining aspect of every Wielder that influences their abilities, strengths, and progression. Classes are generally divided into two categories:
- Combat Classes
- Non-Combat Classes
Classes are acquired during Awakening, when a person successfully forms an Aether Well using an Awakening Stone.
### Combat Classes
Combat classes are classes that allow Wielders to achieve the highest level of combat power for their Rank. Their [[Progression]] is primarily tied to the physical and magical development of the Wielder. With the increase of stats the rank advances and The Wielder becomes more powerful.
### Non-Combat Classes
Non-combat classes are either production classes like [[Blacksmith]] or utility classes like [[Merchant]] and they posses the abilities to create objects for wielders or are able to do certain functions. These classes progress differently since they have 2 ranks. the physical rank, which follows the same rules as Combat Classes, and the Class Rank represents their expertise, knowledge, and proficiency within their profession, and is increased by gaining experience and performing more and more diverse actions related to their Class. An [[Artificer]] can be for example Low-Iron physically and High-Iron at the same time in their Class Rank.
## Subclasses
Subclasses are specialized paths within a Wielder's Class. To obtain a Subclass, a Wielder must reach [[Iron Tier]] and fulfill its specific requirements. Every Subclass grants a Revelation, a spell uniquely suited to its owner, and allows for spells that require certain subclasses. 
The exact Subclass a Wielder obtains is influenced not only by their Class and abilities, but also by their Path, personality, goals, and experiences.
There are subclasses more powerful than others, a way to know it is because certain subclasses never grant revelations below a certain grade.
