# Spell System

## Spell Tiers
Spells have a base tier, human Wielders can only use spells from the same or lower tiers, but some spells can have multiple tiers allowing users to learn them at the spell's lowest tier but have the spell scale in power with the user. This means a powerful [[Copper Tier]] spell may become an equally powerful [[Iron Tier]] spell while keeping the mastery already obtained.
## Spell Grades
Just like Tiers, Spells have Grades that represent the power of said spell within their Tier and the complexity of its grooves. Between each Grade exists a subdivision represented with a + which indicates that a spell has gone beyond the previous grade but not quite reached the next.
Grades are:
- Common
- Uncommon
- Rare
- Epic
- Legendary
- Mythical
## Evolution paths
A spell can be evolved, normally through scrolls but sometimes by carving new grooves, that evolution can change the spell's Tier or its Grade e.g.: [[Shocking Strike]] ([[Copper Tier]] and Uncommon) evolves to [[Dark Shocking Strike]] ([[Copper Tier]] and Rare) or [[Haste]] ([[Copper Tier]] and Uncommon) evolves to [[Huntbound Rush]] ([[Iron Tier]] and Uncommon), this evolution always reduces the user's mastery over the evolved spell compared to the mastery they had over the original one.
## Spell Mastery
 A spell can be mastered which makes it slightly more powerful and more efficient, consuming less aether. The mastery is determined by the refinement of the spell's grooves. The levels of mastery are:
 - Crude
 - Decent
 - Standard
 - Refined
 - Exceptional
 - Flawless
## Spell Alteration
Wielders can alter already existing spells by changing its grooves though doing it requires a deep knowledge of spell theory or risk losing mastery or even unlearn the spell. Alterations may involve changing a spell's elemental affinity or modifying one of its aspects, for example lengthen the duration of the spell at the cost of explosive power. A great alteration could be the fusion of the grooves of 2 different spells to create a single spell that combines the strengths of both spells
## Acquisition Methods
There are basically 3 methods to get a spell from nothing, the most common being scrolls, single-use consumables that allow a Wielder to learn a spell with a certain level of mastery if the restrictions are met. The scrolls are created by [[Scholar]]s either with a Spellshard as a base or with their own knowledge.
Spellshards are beaststones that contain an engraved spell alongside a certain mastery level, functioning similarly to scrolls. But Spellshards can also be used by [[Scholar]]s to try and create a different number of scrolls with the risk of getting nothing, in case the [[Scholar]] manages to learn the secret behind that spell they will be able to keep producing scrolls of that spell given the needed materials.
The last way to learn a spell is by feeling the aether and carving the spell grooves in your body through instinct and sensations, it's the hardest way to learn spells and normally happens faster when in a dangerous situation or under pressure. Beasts use this method much more commonly, despite also being able to use Spellshards they have a much easier time (relative to human Wielders) to just develop spells they need in times of crisis.
## Legacy Spells
[[Legacies]] are a special type of Spellshard containing an extremely powerful and highly scalable spell, usually tied to a specific [[Path]] once followed by its original owner.
When fused with a Wielder, the Legacy forms a Core inside the user's chest. This Core becomes the physical and metaphysical manifestation of the Legacy and the Path connected to it.
The Legacy influences the user to follow a similar Path, slightly altering their personality and psyche over time. The stronger the Legacy, the more pronounced these changes become.
Legacies are typically left behind by immensely powerful Wielders, some of whom are even regarded as Gods. A single individual may leave behind multiple Legacies, although they are usually connected by a common theme or philosophy.
After the death of a Legacy-bearer, the Core can be extracted through a special method. This practice is highly frowned upon, though still secretly performed due to the immense value of Legacies. As a result, possessing a Legacy can be extremely dangerous without proper protection.
A Legacy often grants access to a unique or highly specialized Subclass. However, during the early stages, this Subclass may initially appear as merely a rare or powerful variant until the Wielder reaches higher Tiers.
Legacies are significantly more beneficial to lower-Tier Wielders, as they allow someone to follow a Path different from their natural one. The stronger a Wielder becomes, the more difficult it is to fully adapt to another [[Path]].
Additionally, Legacies greatly facilitate breakthroughs through soft caps and Tier caps, since the Wielder is following a Path that has already been successfully walked before.
