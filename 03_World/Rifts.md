# Rifts

## Overview
Rifts are isolated pocket dimensions separated from the outside world. They are formed around a special plant known as a [[Domain Flower]], which acts as the anchor maintaining the Rift's existence.
Rifts are complete ecosystems that may contain forests, mountains, rivers, deserts, beasts, resources, and even settlements.
They are the primary source of many valuable resources such as [[Seeds]], [[Essences]], [[Spellshards]], and Arcane Beasts.

---
## Domain Flowers
Every Rift is centered around a Domain Flower.
The Tier of the Domain Flower determines the Tier of the Rift itself and limits who may enter it.
The Domain Flower also acts as the anchor of the dimension. If it is harvested or destroyed, the Rift collapses.
When a Rift collapses:
- The Rift ceases to exist.
- Anyone close enough to the Domain Flower may escape before the collapse is complete.
- Anyone too far from the flower is trapped within the collapsing dimension and dies.
Because of this, harvesting a Domain Flower is often considered the final objective of a Rift expedition.

---
## Tier Restrictions
Each Rift possesses a specific Tier determined by its Domain Flower.
Only individuals of the same Tier or lower may enter.
Examples:
- Copper Wielders may enter Copper Rifts.
- Iron Wielders may enter Iron Rifts.
- A Silver Wielder cannot enter an Iron Rift.
These restrictions apply only when entering the Rift.
Once inside, a Wielder or Arcane Beast may continue progressing normally.

---
## Overgrown Rifts
Occasionally, creatures or individuals inside a Rift surpass the Tier of the Domain Flower.
When this occurs, the Rift becomes known as an Overgrown Rift.
Overgrown Rifts are considerably more dangerous than normal Rifts because they may contain inhabitants far stronger than what their entrance restrictions would normally suggest.
As a result, many Overgrown Rifts are considered highly valuable but extremely hazardous.

---
## Portals
Rifts are accessed through portals that connect them to the outside world.
Some Rifts possess only a single portal, while others may have several entrances located in different regions, countries, or even continents.
The number and location of portals vary greatly from one Rift to another.

---
## Escape
The most common method of leaving a Rift without collapsing it is through the use of Escape Scrolls.
These specialized scrolls allow a Wielder to return to the outside world without  harvest the Domain Flower.
Escape Scrolls are considered essential equipment for most Rift expeditions.

---

## Aether Density
Rifts naturally contain significantly higher concentrations of [[Aether]] than the outside world.
The higher the Rift's Tier, the greater its Aether density.
This concentration of Aether accelerates growth, cultivation, and the formation of valuable resources.
As a result, Rifts are among the best locations for training and progression.

---
## Resources
Rifts are the primary source of many valuable resources.
Common resources found within Rifts include:
- [[Seeds]]
- [[Essences]]
- Spellshards
- Beaststones
- Rare materials
- Arcane Beasts
The high concentration of Aether also increases the likelihood of beasts developing and preserving spells within Spellshards.

---
## Settlements
Some large and stable Rifts have become permanently inhabited.
In rare cases, entire cities have been established inside them.
These settlements often develop inside Rifts with multiple portals, allowing them to function as commercial hubs and transportation networks between distant regions of the world.
Certain Rift cities have become major centers of trade due to their strategic locations and access to valuable resources.

---
## Temporal Rifts
Temporal Rifts are a special type of Rift whose portals do not remain permanently open.
Instead, their entrances appear and disappear according to specific cycles or conditions.
When the portal closes, entry becomes impossible until it reopens.
Temporal Rifts can be divided into two broad categories:
### Open-Exit Temporal Rifts
Wielders may leave using Escape Scrolls even while the portal is closed.
These Rifts are generally considered safer and are often used for long-term expeditions.
### Closed-Exit Temporal Rifts
Neither portals nor Escape Scrolls allow exit while the Rift remains closed.
Anyone entering must survive inside until the portal opens again.
These are among the most dangerous known types of Rifts and often require extensive preparation before entry.

---
## Importance
Rifts are among the most strategically important locations in the world.
They provide resources, training opportunities, transportation routes, and access to powerful treasures unavailable elsewhere.
Control over valuable Rifts has shaped the rise and fall of nations, organizations, and powerful Wielders throughout history.
